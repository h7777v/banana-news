Biology is life. Every form of life is made out of cells. There are 2 main types of cells: Prokaryotes and Eukaryotes. Prokaryotes are Cells have a very important thing called enzymes. Basically, enzymes break up or put together different chemicals. This is very important: Each type of enzyme can only deal with a specific type of chemical. They also need a specific environment. Homeostatis is the process that cells use to control things, like the pH level in the cell (see Chemistry for pH. Yes, Biology is just Chemistry). To do this, each cell has a semi-permeable membrane. Small things like oxygen and water can just pass through the membrane, but larger things need to be let through a sort of door that allows things in and out. Naturally, things flow from a place of higher concentration to a place of lower concentration. Water can also flow to a place of higher solute concentration. To flow the other way, you need energy. This energy is called ATP.

How do cells get ATP? Well, animals eat food, which gives them sugar, which has glucose, which added to oxygen can be converted into water, carbon dioxide and ATP. Plants, however, have things called Chloroplasts. Inside the Chloroplast, there's Chlorophyll. These reflect green light (so plants are green) and absorb red and blue light. This light is used to break down water and get carbon dioxide that can be converted to glucose.

Parts of the body

There's a lot to memorise in the body, but I'll start you off with this:

  • Nervous system - Split up into two units, the CNS (Central Nervous System) which is the brain and spinal cord and the PNS (Peripheral Nervous System) which is all other nerves. The Nervous system transmits information from the brain to and from other parts of the body. A sensory input is like a touch or something, it's information that is detected by your sensory system. The PNS will then transmit it to the brain, like "There's a spider on me", and the brain (CNS) will make a decision on what to act. The Motor Output is the response that results from when your brain activates other muscles and glands. In the PNS, there's the motor divison which does what we talked about before, and the sensory division that gets sensory info (like before). In the motor division there is somatic and autonomic (Skeletal movement and automated things like lungs and breathing).
  • Brain - The brain is split up into many parts, such as the cerebral cortex, the cerebellum and the frontal lobe, occipatal lobe, temporal lobe, spinal cord, pons, medula, etc. I will not go into detail with this, but the Cerebral is where most of your conscious thinking happens, while the cerebellum controls more autonomous stuff.

    A population is a group of the same species in a certain area. A community is a group of populations in a certain area. An ecosystem is a group of biotic and abiotic factors which form a system. A biome is a large area with similar climate/physical features that define it.

It includes parasitism, commensalism and mutualism. Parasitism is good for one, bad for the other (+-), commensalism is good for one and neutral for other (+0), while mutualism is good for both (++). Predation is good for predator, bad for prey (+-), which is the same as herbivory (+-)

    Plant hormones

    We've talked about cells, animals and now we're going to talk about plants! What are plant hormones. Basically, Auxins, Gibberlins and Cytokinins are growth hormones. This means they cause cell division

    Auxins cause tropism - directional growth. An example is phototropism: They don't like light, so when light shines on a plant, they move to one side. Now, auxins elongate cells (make them longer), which means that when this happens, one side becomes much longer than the other, sort of pushing the other side over towards the sun.

    Gibberlins cause seed germination and overall stem growth, making tall plants (no gibberlins, you get a dwarfed sort of plant)

    Ethylene aids in fruit ripening

    Abscissic acid causes seed dormancy (keeps seeds not germinating for a while) and creates abscissions (makes dead parts of the plants fall off)

    Hormone Representative Substance Primary Role(s)
    **Auxins** Indole-3-acetic acid (IAA)
    • Promotes cell elongation and primary growth in shoots.
    • Establishes apical dominance (the main stem grows more strongly).
    • Initiates the growth of new roots.
    • Involved in phototropism (growth towards light) and gravitropism (growth in response to gravity).
    **Cytokinins** Zeatin
    • Promotes cell division and differentiation, especially in roots and shoots.
    • Delays the aging process (senescence) of leaves.
    • Inhibits apical dominance, promoting the growth of lateral buds.
    • Involved in embryo and leaf development.
    **Gibberellins (GAs)** Gibberellic acid (GA3)
    • Promotes stem and shoot elongation.
    • Breaks seed and bud dormancy, promoting germination.
    • Stimulates flowering and fruit development.
    • Contributes to bolting (rapid stem elongation before flowering).
    **Abscisic Acid (ABA)** Abscisic acid
    • Induces seed and bud dormancy.
    • Closes stomata in response to water stress to prevent water loss.
    • Counteracts the effects of growth-promoting hormones like gibberellins.
    • Promotes senescence and leaf drop (abscission).
    **Ethylene** Ethylene (a gas)
    • Promotes the ripening of fruit.
    • Accelerates the aging and senescence of flowers and leaves.
    • Contributes to the shedding of leaves, flowers, and fruit (abscission).
    • Induces the "triple response" in seedlings to avoid obstacles.
