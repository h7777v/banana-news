Linear Relationships

Linear Models

Linear relationships describe situations where one quantity changes at a constant rate with respect to another. They can be represented by equations of the form y = mx + c, where m is the gradient (rate of change) and c is the initial value or y-intercept.

Data Analysis

We collect, display, and analyse data to make informed decisions. Measures of centre such as the mean, median, and mode, along with measures of spread like range and interquartile range, help us describe data patterns effectively.

Interpreting Data

We interpret statistical graphs and summaries to compare groups and make conclusions. Outliers can significantly affect the mean and overall interpretation of data.
